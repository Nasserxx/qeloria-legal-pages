We collect the minimum data necessary to authenticate with these platforms and to publish content. We do not sell, share, or rent personal information to any third party.

2. Information We Collect

We collect only the data necessary to authenticate with the integrated platforms and to publish the content you explicitly create or upload. We do not collect any data passively (no analytics SDKs, no advertising identifiers, no fingerprinting).

2.1 Account & authentication data

  • TikTok: OAuth 2.0 access token, refresh token, open_id, and the scopes you authorized (currently: user.info.basic, video.publish, video.upload).
  • Facebook: OAuth 2.0 user access token, app-scoped user ID, and the permissions you granted (currently: pages_show_list, pages_manage_posts, pages_read_engagement, pages_manage_engagement, public_profile, email).
  • Instagram (via Facebook Graph): IG user/business account ID linked to the authorized Facebook Page, and a Page access token with the instagram_basic, instagram_content_publish, and instagram_manage_insights permissions.

2.2 Content data you provide

  • Video files (mp4) you upload for publishing.
  • Text and metadata: title, caption, description, hashtags, scheduled publish time, thumbnail preference.
  • Publishing configuration: which TikTok / Facebook Page / Instagram account to post to, the schedule calendar, and your draft-vs-publish choices.

2.3 Operational data

  • Publish logs (timestamps, post IDs returned by each platform, success/failure status).
  • Application errors (sanitized — no tokens or passwords ever logged).

2.4 What we DO NOT collect

  • Your TikTok / Facebook / Instagram password (we never see it; OAuth is used).
  • Private messages, friend lists, followers, or contacts.
  • Browsing history, device identifiers, advertising IDs, or location data.
  • Biometric, financial, or government-identification data.
  • Data from users who have not authorized our app.

4. Third-Party Services & API Permissions

To publish on your behalf, our backend servers make calls to the following third-party APIs. Your use of each platform is governed by that platform's own terms.

4.1 TikTok — Content Posting API

  • Endpoint providers: TikTok (ByteDance Ltd.)
  • Permissions used: user.info.basic (read display name, avatar), video.publish (publish to your account), video.upload (upload source file).
  • Data shared with TikTok: video file, caption, hashtags, and the access token identifying you.
  • Data received from TikTok: post ID, publish status, basic profile (display name, open_id).
  • Privacy policy: https://www.tiktok.com/legal/privacy-policy

4.2 Meta (Facebook & Instagram) — Graph API

  • Endpoint providers: Meta Platforms, Inc.
  • Facebook permissions used: public_profile, email, pages_show_list, pages_manage_posts, pages_read_engagement, pages_manage_engagement.
  • Instagram permissions used (via Business Login): instagram_basic, instagram_content_publish, instagram_manage_insights.
  • Data shared with Meta: video file, caption, the target Page ID / IG user ID, and the access token identifying you.
  • Data received from Meta: post ID, publish status, Page name, Page ID, IG user ID, and basic profile (name, email if granted).
  • Privacy policy: https://www.facebook.com/privacy/policy/
  • Instagram-specific: https://privacycenter.instagram.com/policy

4.3 No other third parties

We do not embed any analytics, advertising, or social-tracking SDKs. We do not share your data with data brokers, marketing partners, or any other third party not listed above.

5. Data Storage & Security

  • OAuth tokens are stored encrypted at rest (AES-256) on private servers with restricted access.
  • All HTTP traffic uses TLS 1.2+ (HTTPS).
  • Video files are stored only as long as needed to complete the publish job (typically deleted within 24 hours of successful upload).
  • Access to production data is restricted to the app operator and protected by multi-factor authentication.
  • We perform periodic security reviews and rotate keys on a documented schedule.

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. If a breach occurs, we will notify affected users and the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours, in line with GDPR Art. 33.

6. Data Retention

  • OAuth tokens: retained until you revoke access or delete your account. Refresh tokens are deleted on logout.
  • Video files: deleted within 24 hours of a successful publish, or immediately upon a failed publish.
  • Publish logs (post ID, status, timestamp): retained for 12 months for debugging, then anonymized.
  • Support emails: retained for 24 months, then deleted.

7. Your Rights

Regardless of where you live, you have the following rights:

  • Access: request a copy of all data we hold about you.
  • Correction: ask us to fix inaccurate data.
  • Deletion: ask us to erase all your data — performed within 30 days.
  • Portability: receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Object / restrict processing: opt out of any optional processing.
  • Withdraw consent: at any time, with no retroactive effect.
  • Lodge a complaint: with your local data-protection authority.

7.1 How to exercise these rights

Email awadbot1@gmail.com with the subject line "Privacy Request". We verify identity before acting and respond within 30 days.

7.2 How to revoke platform access (Meta & TikTok requirements)

  • TikTok: TikTok app → Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Security → Manage app permissions → Qeloria Content Publisher → Remove.
  • Facebook: Settings & privacy → Settings → Business integrations → Qeloria Content Publisher → Remove.
  • Instagram: Settings → Account Center → Your information and permissions → Apps and websites → Qeloria Content Publisher → Remove.

Revoking access through the platform immediately invalidates our stored tokens. We additionally delete the token record from our database within 24 hours.
